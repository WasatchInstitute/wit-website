Life at WIT

Learning sessions

Learning sessions are instructional sessions focused on a specific learning objective(s). These sessions should engage the students in discovering the ideas associated with each objective and the connections between objectives as they apply what they have learned to accomplish meaningful work. Frequently this will be done through problem-based learning and hands-on activities.

Learning sessions are typically led by a Teaching Team member. The duration of a learning session may be adjusted for each sprint, depending on the time required by the learning objective. Learning sessions are not traditional academic courses, since they regularly cross subject area boundaries and no grade is assigned to the learning session.

Work sessions

Work sessions are periods of focused work during a sprint designed to help students complete their sprint deliverables. Work sessions, often occurring during the last week of the sprint, have been effective for our students as it gives them a chance to improve their work.

Students who have already completed their sprint deliverables before the work sessions have opportunities to go on additional field trips or participate in other projects at the school, such as wiring the building, assembling computers, configuring the weather station, or other interesting things.



Lunch at WIT

45 minutes to see the world

Lunch time

Lunch at WIT generally starts at 11:30 each day and is 45 minutes long, although the lunch duration and starting times may vary due to activities or field trips. During the lunch period, students are encouraged to read, exercise, or work on their schoolwork in addition to taking a break.

Refrigerators and microwaves are available for students to use. Additionally there are several restaurants nearby easily accessible to students. Students can go wherever they like for lunch, so long as they are back to WIT on time.

Popular lunch spots

Lunch Study Hall

Lunch study hall is a place where students can get some extra help with their sprint deliverables or character development during lunch. During this time, the teacher helps each student develop skills in reading, writing, organization, studying, prioritization, making decisions, reflection, behavior, and personal hygiene--among others.

Our goal is to equip students with skills and tools to help them be the best they can be, enabling them to improve and ultimately solve their own problems.

Guest speakers

Guest speakers share their expertise with WIT

Each sprint, WIT has guest speakers come in to share their background and experience with us as it relates to the theme's sprint. These guest speakers are working professionals with significant experience and background information. By incorporating these speakers, we're expanding our knowledge beyond the classroom. Just look at who we've had speak to us so far:

  • restaurant_menuFood sprint

    During our food sprint, we learned about how our food is grown and processed. We had a speaker from OSI speak about meat processing, the Utah Dairy Council speak about milk production and family farms, and the Salt Lake County Health Department speak about restaurant inspections.

  • tabletMedia

    Jennifer Napier-Pearce of the Salt Lake Tribune spoke to us about the power of the media, both historically and currently. As a former radio reporter and newspaper columnist, Jennifer was able to share with us her unique insights into radio and newspaper news. Spy Hop Productions also came to WIT to help our students learn to create videos.

  • albumMusic

    Members of the Utah Opera spoke to us about the history of opera, how opera works today, and performed some catchy pieces for us.

  • local_drinkWater

    The individual in charge of wastewater permissing for the Utah Division of Water Quality spoke to us about the wastewater permitting process. Theron Kingston, an amazing local ice sculptor, came to WIT to carve our logo out of ice right before our eyes.

  • radioRoaring Twenties

    One of the student lead developers from the University of Utah's Gaming Program came to WIT to describe the process of creating Cyber Heist, the 2014 IGF Student Winner.

  • account_balanceEuropean Renaissance

Why we do it

Daily cleaning is an important routine at WIT, and helps us turn the abstract concept of "responsibility" into a concrete set of actions. We take the time to show our students the proper way to clean and then help to make them successful at it. Each WIT advisory class takes a turn each week of the month to clean the common areas of the building, while all advisories are responsible for cleaning their advisory room.

Looking at the big picture, we hope to teach our students that doing chores is a part of life and that there is in fact pleasure in taking care of our things. Knowing how to keep things clean is an important life skill, and hopefully will teach them to think twice about where they stick their used gum!
