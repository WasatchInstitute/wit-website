GIRLS IN IT/DIVERSITY STATEMENT

Workers in IT are overwhelmingly male. However, IT is one of the only places in which the wage gap between white female and male employees with the same qualifications isn't $0.77 to $1. So, you may ask what WIT's stance is on females. While our student body does not represent it, we want female students to get into technology. Even while they aren't typically pushed towards STEM fields, they're just as intelligent as their male peers. We want female students, and employers want female employees.


WHAT TO EXPECT

LThe day starts at 8:30 with a fifteen-minute advisory, and then classes begin (including SCOPs). Lunch is from 11:30-12:30, and then classes resume until 3:15, with afternoon advisory and the day ends at 3:30.


Starting at WIT, you should know that you need to take charge of your actions and be very responsible. The teachers won't nag you to get your work in if you don't do it. You should expect freedom, but you should also expect to be held accountable for your actions and face the consequences (i.e. less time at luch, not picking your classes, etc.).

GETTING TO WIT

TRAX is how we get most everywhere. Getting to school is no exception. The school is not in close enough proximity for any students to walk, and driving your student every morning can be very incovenient for parents. Luckily, thants to Frontrunner and the bus, WIT can be accessed from more or less everywhere in the valley. A lot of students were skepticall at first, but once they got into it and started to understand it, it hasn't been hard at all.


LOCATION

WIT is located in Murray, right between the Murray central TRAX and Frontrunner stations. WHile the plan is to move, the location is actually pretty ideal. DUe to its spot right in the middle of our valley, we have a good way to get anywhere from the University of Utah to BYU. Our future location is in Daybreak.


SHADOWING/VISITING FOR A DAY

If you want to visit for the day or give shadowing a try, please contact the main office and make plans in advance. Drop in visits can be a problem due to the fact that we go on a lot of field trips and many not be at the school at any given time.
